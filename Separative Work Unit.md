The standadr measure for of the effort required to enrich [[Uranium]]-235 for nuclear fuel, representing the quantity of separative work performed by an enrichmnt enrichment plant.
- It's a measure of energy-intensive service (kg/year), not a unit of energy itself.
- One SWU corresponds to 1kg of separative work, measuring the separation of U235 from U238.
- Note that natural uranium contains only 0.7% U235, but most commercial [[Light Water Reactor|LWR]]s require 3-5% enrichment, requiring input of separative work.
- Approximately 100,000 SWU is required to fuel a typical 1000MWe commercial nuclear reactor for one year.



Major enrichment companies like Orano and JNFL manage millions of SWU per year for global nuclear capacity.
