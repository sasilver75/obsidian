A primary staff member in military units ([[Battalion]] level and above) responsible for supply chain management, maintenance, and overall logistical support.

They ensure that the unit has the equipment, food, fuel, and transport needed to function effectively in both garrison and field environments.

Core responsibilities:
- Supply Management: Tracking/ordering/distributing equipment and supplies using tools like [[GCSS-Army]] 
- Maintenance Tracking: Overseeing vehicle and equipment maintenance schedules and reporting operational readiness.
- Budget Oversight: Managing the unit's logistics budget and processing financial 

Works alongside other primary [[Staff Officer]]s to help commanders make informed decisions.

### Comparison with [[Support Operations Officer]]s (SPOs)
- An [[S4 Officer]] manages ***==internal*** unit logistics==, supply, and property accountability.
- A [[Support Operations Officer|SPO]] plans, coordinates, and synchronizes ***==external sustainment support==*** , acting as the primary liaison between supported combat units and higher-[[Army Echelons|Echelon]] logistics battalions.
- ==Commodity Managers== oversee specific [[Class of Supply]]s (e.g. ammunition, medical materiel, or electronics) through the entire lifecycle, responsible for forecasting demands, mitigating supply chain disruptions, and optimizing inventory management in context.


### What's the difference between the Brigade S4 and the SPO? (Pod [LINK](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n9AjjdtHWlA))
- In Amy Beatty's mind, clearly distinguished:
	- Brigade S4: 
		- Manages ==internal sustainment==. Manages admin.
		- [[Logistics Status|LOGSTAT]]-dependent. Needs to focus on internal requirements.
	- Support Operations officer: 
		- Manages ==external sustainment==. Commander-esque, because they have all commodities, and coordinate with companies to support the light fighter.
		- Ties to the outside world... [[Combat Sustainment Support Battalion|CSSB]] and [[Division Sustainment Support Battalion|DSSB]]... 
		- You live on the synchronization matrix... This becomes your number one fighting product. Re: the "SPO Shop," when the SPO ends up picking up work from the Brigade S4, is because the SPO has a bigger shop. They have [[Commodity Manager]]s. 
			- The S4 has a handful of people, and when you're forward-deployed, their shop isn't necessarily large enough to do commodity management for every commodity for every battalion.
			- But the SPO has commodity managers... if you'er just looking at Class 1, 5, 3... any one of those can manage, at depth, all of the Battalions specific to their commodity.
			- So when we submit [[Logistics Status|LOGSTAT]]s from maneuver all the way up through the Battalion S4s and then up to Brigade, when it splits off and is redundantly reported to the SPO, the SPO ends up picking up the work that *should be* the Brigade S4 because the Brigade S4 isn't built in depth for management/assessment in the way that the SPO is. The SPO has all the horsepower -- commodity management and tracking often falls back to them, because they have the people to do it.
- One position is not more/less likely than another to have a stronger logistician in it, it comes down to the brigade commander's preference. 
- If the Brigade SPO and the Brigade S4 aren't working together and tracking the same requirements throughout the fight, there's going to be things that slip through the cracks




# Comparison with [[S4 Officer]]
- [[S4 Officer]] is the logistics staff officer for the **supported unit**.
	- "What does my unit need to keep operating?"
	- Internally focused
- [[Support Operations Officer]] is the logistics officer for the **supporting sustainment unit**.
	- "How do we provide support to all of the units that we're responsible for?"
	- Externally focused
- Simply:
	- S4: Logistics staff inside the unit being supported
	- SPO: Support planner inside the sustainment unit providing support


Note that You see both [[S4 Officer|S4]]s and [[Support Operations Officer|SPO]]s at multiple tactical echelons.

For S4's, you commonly see:
- [[Battalion]] [[S4 Officer|S4]] officer: Logistics officer for a battalion
- [[Brigade]] [[S4 Officer|S4]] officer: Logistics officer for a brigade
- Sometimes Regiment equivalents, depending on organization
The S4 works for that unit's commander and manages that unit's logistics planning, reporting, sustainment coordination, property/accountability concerns, and requirements.

For SPOs, you commonly see them in:
- [[Brigade Support Battalion]]s (BSBs)
- [[Light Support Battalion]]s (LSB)
- [[Combat Sustainment Support Battalion]] (CSSBs)
- [[Division Sustainment Support Battalion]] (DSSBs)
- [[Division Sustainment Brigade]] (DSBs)
- [[Expeditionary Sustainment Command]]s  (ESCs)
The SPO works for the sustainment commander, and coordinates support to customer/supported units.
