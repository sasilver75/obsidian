The act of searching for [[Wi-Fi]] wireless networks as well as cell towers, usually from a moving vehicle, using a laptop or smartphone.

Variants: Warbiking, Warcycling.

Wardrivers use a Wi-Fi equipped device together with a [[Global Positioning System|GPS]] device to record the location of wireless networks. The result can then be uploaded to sites like WiGLE, openBmap, or Geomena, where data is processed to form maps of the network neighborhood.

These maps of known network IDs can then be used as a [[Geolocation]] system, an alternative to GPS, by triangulating the current position from the signal strengths of known network IDs.
