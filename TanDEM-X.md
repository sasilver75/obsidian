"[[TerraSAR-X]] add-on for [[Digital Elevation Model]] measurements)", a German radar mission in partnership with Airbus.

Consists of two nearly identical [[Synthetic Aperture Radar|SAR]] satellites:
- [[TerraSAR-X]] (2007)
- [[TanDEM-X]] (2010)
==Flying in close formation, separated by only a few hundred meters to a few kilometers==.

The ==formation flying is the key innovation==; like [[Shuttle Radar Topography Mission|SRTM]]'s 60meter boom, having two antennas at slightly different positions at the same moment gives you [[Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar|Single-Pass InSAR]]!
- Unlike SRTM's 11-day mission, [[TanDEM-X]] has been collecting data continuously since 2010.

The ==primary product== was the [[WorldDEM]], a ==global [[Digital Elevation Model|DEM]] at 12m resolution== with ~2 relative vertical accuracy and ~10m absolute vertical accuracy.
- ==Significantly better than SRTM in both resolution and accuracy.==
- I believe (2026) ==this is the highest resolution global DEM==, with the best free/public one being Copernicus [[Copernicus DEM|GLO-30]], which is derived from this.
