A detailed military plan for how a commander intends to conduct a possible operation.
- See also: [[Operational Order|OPORD]]
	- Common relationship: OPLAN -> execution decision -> OPORD
	- OPLAN is often used as the basis for an OPORD, once the commander decides to conduct an operation.

More developed than a rough concept of contingency idea, but not necessarily directly executable. Includes:
- Mission and commander's intent
- Situation and assumptions
- Concept of operations ([[Concept of Operations|CONOP]])
- Task organization
- Tasks to subordinate units
- Coordinating instructions
- Sustainment plan
- Command and signal instructions
- Annexes for [[Intelligence]], [[Fires]], [[Sustainment|Logistics]], Communications, [[Protection]], etc.
