
Two donut-shaped zones of charged particles (electrons and protons) trapped by Earth's magnetic field.
- ==Inner== belt: ==~1,000-12,000km altitude==, mostly high-energy protons, relatively stable.
- ==Outer== belt: ==~15,000-25,000 km altitude==. Mostly high-energy electrons. Highly dynamic, swells and shrinks with solar activity.

They exist because the Earth's magnetic field acts as a trap; charged particles get capture and spiral back and forth along magnetic field lines between the poles, unable to escape.

Relevance for Satellites:
- ==They damage Electronics==, causing bit flips, latch-up, long-term degradation.
- Solar panels' efficiency degrades over time.

Satellite orbits are designed around this:
- [[Low Earth Orbit]] (~400km): Below the inner belt, mostly safe.
- [[Medium Earth Orbit]] (~20,000km), where [[Global Positioning System|GPS]] lives): Sits right in the belts; need radiation hardening.
- [[Geostationary Orbit]] (~36,000km): Above the worst of it, but still exposed to the outer belt.
