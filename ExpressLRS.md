A highly popular open-source radio control (RC) link protocol primarily used for FPV [[Drone]]s, RC planes, and long-range UAVs.

Key features:
- Extreme Range: Supports both 2.4GHz and 900MHz frequencies. In ideal conditions, can achieve ranges of 50-100km, depending on output power and frequency.
- Ultra-Low Latency: Designed for lightning-fast responsiveness, it supports packet rates up to 1000 Hz, making it highly responsive for aggressive acrobatic flying.
- Open-Source: Because it is community-driven and not proprietary, hardware from various companies is highly compatible and significantly more affordable than older, locked-in systems.
- Allows for two-way communication, so you can send vital data like battery voltage back to your controller screen or goggles.
