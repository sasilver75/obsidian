Short for: Phased Array type L-band [[Synthetic Aperture Radar]]
- A Japanese radar instrument (not optical), so it sees through clouds and captures surface/subsurface structure rather than reflected light.

### What makes it distinctive: L-band Radar
- [[Synthetic Aperture Radar|L-Band]] (~24cm wavelength) is long, compared to other common [[Synthetic Aperture Radar|SAR]], so penetrates vegetation canopy and interacts with large woody structures (trunks, branches), not just leaves.
	- Compare this with the [[Synthetic Aperture Radar|C-Band]] radar used in [[Sentinel|Sentinel-1]], which only does shallow canopy penetration.

Dual Polarization modes:
- PALSAR transmits/receives in combinations of H (horizontal) and V (vertical):
	- HH: horizontal transmit, horizontal receive, sensitive to surface/trunk scattering
	- HV: horizontal transmit, vertical receive, sensitive to volume scattering (canopy)
	- HH/HV together: Much richer forest structure signal than either alone.
