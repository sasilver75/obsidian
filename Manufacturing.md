The organized process of turning raw materials, components, labor, equipment, and know-how into finished goods.


The four foundational manufacturing processes that are used to shape and build materials:
- ==[[Formative Process]]==
	- Mechanical forces, pressure, or heat are applied to coerce and shape a material, without adding or removing any mass.
	- Best for high-volume mass production; requires high initial costs for tooling (like [[Molding|Mold]]s), but yields a very low cost-per-part.
	- Key methods:
		- [[Molding]]
		- [[Injection Molding]]
		- [[Casting]]: Liquid is poured into a mold and solidified.
		- [[Forging]]: Metal is shaped by compressive force, often improving strength.
		- [[Stamping]]: Dies press/cut/form material into shape.
		- [[Bending]]
- ==[[Additive Process]]==
	- Material is built up layer-by-layer to create 3D objects from a digital file
	- Best used for complex, highly-customized, or lightweight geometries that would be too difficult or expensive to carve or cast.
	- Key methods:
		- [[3D Printing]]
		- [[Selective Laser Sintering]]
		- [[Fused Deposition Modeling]]
- ==[[Subtractive Process]]==
	- Material is removed or "carved" from a larger solid block ([[Billet]]) until the desired shape is achieved.
	- Best used for larger, high-precision, less complex parts and materials that are tough to 3D print (e.g. solid metals, certain woods, and stones).
	- Key methods:
		- [[CNC Machining]]: Computer-controlled cutting of material using tools like [[Milling|Mill]]s, [[Turning|Lathe]]s, and [[Drilling|Drill]]s
			- e.g. [[Routing]]
		- [[Milling]]: A machining process where rotating cutting tools remove material from a workpiece
		- [[Turning]]: A workpiece rotates while a cutting tool shapes it.
		- [[Drilling]]
		- [[Grinding]]: A precision material-removal process using (e.g.) an abrasive wheel.
- ==Joining Process== (Sometimes not considered a manufacturing process like with the ones above)
	- Multiple discrete parts or distinct materials are assembled and permanently or semi-permanently fastened together to build a complete product.
	- Best used for large structures (bridges, buildings, car frames) and assemblies that require a mix of different materials or components.
	- Key methods:
		- [[Welding]]: A joining process that fuses materials, usually metals, using heat, pressure, or both.
		- [[Brazing]]: A joining process where filler metal bonds parts without melting the base materials.
		- [[Riveting]]: A mechanical joining process using permanent metal fasters called rivets.
		- [[Bolting]]
		- [[Bonding|Adhesive Bonding]]: A joining process that uses glue or chemical adhesives to attach parts.

Additional Terms:
- [[Fixture]]: A device that holds a [[Workpiece]] in a repeatable position during manufacturing.
- [[Jig]]: A device that guides a tool or operation while holding a part in place.
- [[Bill of Materials]] (BOM): The structured list of parts, materials, and subassemblies needed to make a product.
- [[Die]]: A specialized tool used to cut, stamp, forge, or form material.
- [[Tolerance]]: The allowed variation from a specified dimension or property.
	- "Tolerance Stack-Up"
- [[Yield]]: The percentage of output that meets requirements without scrap or rework.
- [[Scrap Rate]]: The percentage of output that DOES NOT meet requirements, requiring scrapping (or rework?)
- [[Throughput]]: The rate at which a system produces finished output.
- [[Cycle Time]]: The time required to complete one unit or operation.
- [[Takt Time]] (Takt): The pace of production required to meet customer demand.
- [[Rework]]: The additional work required to fix a defective or nonconforming part.
- [[Setup Time]]: The time needed to prepare equipment or tooling for a production run.
- [[Changeover]]: The process of switching a production line or machine from one product to another.
- [[Lead Time]]: The total time from order, request, or start to completion or delivery.
- [[Lot Size]]: The number of units produced or processed together.
- [[Batch Size]]
- [[Bottleneck]]: The process step that limits total output.
- [[Draft Angle]]: A slight taper added to molded or cast parts so they release from the mold.
- [[Undercut]]
- [[Parting Line]]: The line on a molded or cast part where two mold halves meet.
- [[Rib]] (injection molding)
- [[Boss]] (i.e. protruding feature on a workpiece)

- [[Truing]]
- [[Fillet]]
- [[Chamfer]]
- [[Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing]] (GD&T): A symbolic engineering language for specifying allowable geometric variation in parts.
- Design Freeze
- Production: The act of making goods, emphasizing output volume, schedule, efficiency.
- Factory: A facility where people, machines, materials, and processes are arranged to produce goods repeatedly.
- [[Work Cell]]: A grouped set of machines, tools, and workers arranged around a specific production task or product family.
- Assembly line: A production system where work moves through a fixed sequence of stations.
- [[Job Shop]]: A flexible manufacturing environment that handles varied, often custom low-volume work.
- [[Flow Production]]: A high-volume approach where products move continuously through standardized steps.
- [[Batch Production]]: A production method where a set quantity of similar items is made, before switching to another item.
- [[High-Mix Low-Volume]]: Manufacturing many product variants in relatively small quantities.
- [[Low-Mix High-Volume]]: Manufacturing a small number of product types in very large quantities.
- [[Datum]]: A reference point, line, or plane used to define part geometry or measurements.
- [[Tooling]]: Custom tools, [[Molding|Mold]]s, [[Die]]s. [[Fixture]]s, or equipment needed to make a part.
- [[Molding|Mold]]: A cavity or form used to shape material during casting, molding, or forming.
- [[Molding]]
	- Cavity: The hollow space in a mold that forms the final part shape.
	- Ejector pin: A pin that pushes a molded part out of the mold after cooling.
	- Sink mark: A surface depression caused by uneven cooling or thick material sections.
	- Knit line: A visible or weal line where two flows of molten plastic meet.
	- Short shot: An incomplete molded part caused by insufficient material filling the mold.
	- Warpage: Distortion caused by uneven cooling, internal stress, or poor part design.
	- Gate vestige: the small mark left where material entered a molded part.
	- Mold flow: The movement of molten plastic through a mold cavity.
	- Clamping force: The force holding mold halves closed during injection molding.
	- Residence time: The time material spends heated inside a molding machine before injection.
- [[Capacity]]: The maximum amount a factory, line, or machine can produce in a given time.
- [[Utilization]]: The percentage of available [[Capacity]] actually being used.
- [[Work in Process]] (WIP): Partially completed goods moving through production.
- [[Scrap]]: Material or parts that cannot be used or sold because they fail requirements.
- [[Overall Equipment Effectiveness]] (OEE): A metric combining equipment availability, performance, and quality.
- [[Kaizen]]: Continuous improvement through small, ongoing changes.
- [[5S]]: A workplace organization method: sort, set in order, shine, standardize, and sustain.
- [[Kanban]]: A visual signal system used to manage work and inventory flow.
- [[Poka-yoke]] ("POH-kah YOH-kay"):  A Japanese concept meaning "mistake proofing" or "error prevention", refers to any design/mechanism, or procedure that makes it impossible for an inadvertent human error to occur, or makes the error immediately obvious so it can be corrected.
- Andon: A visual or audible alert system for production problems.
- Gemba (Japanese: "Place"): The shop floor where value is created. Refers to philosophy of leaders stepping out of offices to observe work firsthand, engage with operators, identify waste, and drive continuous improvement.
- Near-net Shape: A part made close to its final geometry, requiring little finishing.
- Net shape: A part made directly to final geometry with little or no post-processing.
- [[Burr]]: A thin unwanted edge or raised bit of metal left on apart for later machining or finishing.
- [[Deburring]]: The process of removing burrs from a manufactured part.
- [[Toolpath]]: The programmed route a cutting tool follows while machining a part.
- [[Feed Rate]]: The speed at which a cutting tool advances through a material.
- [[Chatter]]: Unwanted vibration during machining that can damage surface finish and tools.
- [[Tapping]]: Cutting internal screen threads into a hole.
- [[Threading]]: Creating screw threads on internal or external surface
- [[Broaching]]: Removing material with a toothed tool that cuts a specific profile.
- [[Honing]]: Abrasive finishing used to improve hole geometry and surface finish.
- [[Lapping]]: Fine abrasive finishing used to create very flat or smooth surfaces.
- [[Knurling]]: Creating a textured pattern on a surface, often for grip.
- [[Shearing]]: Cutting sheet metal using opposing blades
- [[Punching]]: Creating holes or shapes in sheet metal with a [[Punch]] and [[Die]]
- [[Blanking]]: Cutting a flat shape out of sheet metal for later forming.
- [[Piercing]]: Punching holes or openings into a sheet-metal part.
- [[Bending]]: Deforming material along a straight axis to create an angle.
- [[Bend Radius]]: The inside radius of a bend in sheet metal or formed material.
- Springback: The tendency of metal to partially return to its original shape after forming.
- Deep drawing: Forming sheet metal into deep hollow shapes using a punch and a die.
- Roll forming: Passing sheep metal through rollers to gradually form a long, continuous profile.
- [[Hydroforming]]: Forming metal using pressurized fluid instead of a rigid die surface.
- Swaging: Shaping metal by compressing it with dies, often to reduce diameter or form ends.
- [[Coining]]: A high-pressure forming process that creates precise details or sharp features.
- Progressive die: A die system that performs multiple sheet-metal operations as material advances through stations.

Heat and surface treatment:
- [[Annealing]]: Heat treatment that softens material and reduces internal stress.
- [[Quenching]]: Rapid cooling used to harden some materials after heating.
- [[Tempering]]: Reheating hardened metal to reduce brittleness and improve toughness.
- [[Case Hardening]]: Hardening only the outer surface of a metal part.
- Stress Reliving: Heat treatment used to reduce internal stresses from forming, welding, or machining.
- [[Anodizing]]: An electrochemical surface treatment that improves corrosion resistance, especially for aluminum.
- [[Plating]]: Depositing a thin metal coating on a part surface.
- Passivation: Chemical treatment that improves corrosion resistance, especially for stainless steel.
- [[Powder Coating]]: Applying dry powder to a surface and curing it into a durable coating.
- Shot peening: Bombarding a surface with small media to improve fatigue resistance.
- [[Bead Blasting]]: Cleaning or texturing a surface using small abrasive particles.


Supply chain and Business

- [[Request for Quote]] (RFQ): A formal request for pricing and terms from suppliers.
- [[Minimum Order Quantity]] (MOQ): The smallest quantity a supplier will sell or produce.
- [[Single Sourcing]]: Relying on a single supplier for a part or material
- [[Dual Sourcing]]: Relying on two suppliers to reduce supply risk.
- [[Reshoring]]: Moving production back to the home country.
- [[Nearshoring]]: Moving production closer to the final market.
- [[Safety Stock]]: Extra inventory held to protect against uncertainty.

- [[Total Landed Cost]]: The full cost of getting a product delivered, including freight, duties, inventory, and risk.

Manufacturing Quality terms:
- Quality Assurance: Systems and processes designed to prevent defects.
- Quality Control: Inspection and testing activities used to detect defects.
- Inspection: Checking a part/product/process against requirements.
- [[Metrology]]: The science and practice of measurement.
- Gauge: A tool used to measure or verify a dimension or feature.
	- Go/No-Go Gauge: A simple gauge telling whether a part is within acceptable limits.
- [[Coordinate Measuring Machine]] (CMM): Equipment that precisely measures part geometry.
- Calibration: The process of verifying and adjusting measurement controls against a known standard.
 - [[Statistical Process Control]] (SPC): Using data and control charts to monitor process stability.
 - Process capability: A measure of how well a process can produce within tolerance.
 - Cp/Cpk: Statistical metrics showing how capable and centered a process is, relative to specifications.
 - FAI: First Article Inspection, a formal inspection of the first production part or batch.
 - [[Root Cause Analysis]]: A structured effort to find the underlying cause of a problem.
 - [[Correct Action]]: A fix intended to eliminate the cause of a defect or failure.
 - NDT: Non-destructive testing, inspection methods that evaluate parts without damaging them.
 - ISO 9001: A widely-used international quality management standard; a standard for creating and maintaining a [[Quality Management System]] (QMS).


Welding and Joining
- [[Weld Bead]]: The deposited line of fused material created during welding.
- Heat-Affected Zone: The area near a weld whose properties changed because of heat.
- Penetration: The depth to which a weld fuses into the base material.
- Filler Metal: Added metal used to fill a weld joint.
- Shielding gas: Gas used to protect molten weld metal from air contamination.
- [[Tack Weld]]: A small temporary weld used to hold parts in position before final welding.
- Weld fixture: A [[Fixture]] that holds parts in correct alignment during welding.
- Distortion: Unwanted warping caused by heating, cooling, forming, or residual stress.
- Preheating: Heating material before welding or forming to reduce cracking or stress.
- Post-weld heat treatment: Heat treatment after welding to reduce stress or improve properties.

Molding and Plastics:
- Shot size: The amount of material injected into a mold during one molding cycle.
- Pack pressure: Pressure applied after filling a mold to compensate for shrinkage.
- Hold time: The time pressure is maintained after injection to improve part quality.
- Cooling time: The time a molded part remains in the mold while solidifying.
- Melt temperature: The temperature of molten plastic before injection.
- Mold temperature: The temperature of the mold surfaces during molding.
- Venting: Small pathways that let trapped air escape from a mold.
- Runner system: Channels that carry molten plastic from the machine nozzle to part cavities.
- Hot runner: A heated runner system that keeps plastic molten inside the mold.
- Cold runner: A runner system where plastic solidifies and is ejected with the part.


Additive Manufacturing
- Build plate: The platform on which a 3D printed part is formed.
- Support structure: Temporary printed material used to hold overhands during printing.
- Infill: The internal pattern or density inside a 3D-printed part.
- Layer height: The thickness of each deposited or fused layer in additive manufacturing.
- Build orientation: The direction a part is positioned during printing, affecting strength and finish.
- Slicing: Converting a 3D model into printable layers and machine instructions.
- Post-cure: Additional curing after printing to reach final material properties.
- Powder bed: A layer of powder used in additive processes such as SLS or metal laser fusion.


Assembly:
- [[Subassembly]]: A smaller assembled unit that later becomes part of a larger product.
- Kitting: Preparing all required parts and materials for a build or assembly operation.
- Torque spec: The required tightening force for a fastener.
- Threadlocker: A chemical adhesive used to prevent threaded fasteners from loosening.
- [[Press fit]]: A joint where parts are held together by interference rather than fasteners.
- [[Slip fit]]: A joint where parts slide together with small clearance.
- [[Interference fit]]: A fit where one part is slightly larger than the mating space, creating a tight hold.
- [[Clearance fit]]: A fit where parts have intentional space between them for easy movement or assembly.
