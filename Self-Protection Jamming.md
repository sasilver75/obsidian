Using an aircraft's internal or podded [[Electronic Warfare]] (EW) systems to actively disrupt hostile tracking and targeting radars. 
- SPJ systems combine noise, deception, and digital radio frequency memory (DRFM) to break a threat's radar lock, in an effort to ensure the host aircraft's survival.

Typically focus on defending the host aircraft from late-stage engagement threats (e.g. surface to air missile batteries or enemy fighter radars).
