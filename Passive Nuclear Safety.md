

A design approach for safety features in a [[Nuclear Reactor]] that ==does not require any active intervention on the part of the operator or electrical/electronic feedback in order to bring the reactor to a safe shutdown state== in the event of a particular type of emergency (typically overheating).

Many older common reactor designs use passive safety systems only in a limited extend, instead relying on [[Active Nuclear Safety]] systems such as diesel-powered motors.

Newer reactor designs use more passive systems, with the motivation being that they're more reliable and reduce the cost associated with the installation and maintenance of systems that would otherwise require multiple trains of equipment and redundant safety-class power supplies.
