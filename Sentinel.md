Part of the EU [[Copernicus DEM|Copernicus]] program, Europe's [[Remote Sensing|EO]] program run by [[European Space Agency]] (ESA). A family of purpose-built satellite missions, each designed for a specific observation type. All data is free and open access, a deliberate decision that transformed EO research and industry. US followed  a philosophy with [[Landsat]] imagery in 2008.

A satellite mission that acquires optical imagery at high spatial resolutions (10m to 60m) over land and coastal waters.

==Sentinel-1==: [[Synthetic Aperture Radar|C-Band]] [[Synthetic Aperture Radar|SAR]]
- Two satellites, 1A (2014) and 1B( 2016, failed 2022), 1B replaced by 1C in 2024
- Primary uses are surface deformation ([[InSAR]]), flood mapping, sea ice, ship detection, agriculture
- 6 day repaet

==Sentinel-2==: [[Multispectral]] Optical
- The workhorse of free [[Remote Sensing|EO]] data.
- Two satellites, 2A (2015) and 2B (2017)
- Captures 13 spectral bands (shown below)
	- The red edge bands (B5, B6, B7) are Sentinel-2's distinct advantage over [[Landsat]]; these bands are particularly sensitive to chlorophyll content and plant stress, which is important for precision agriculture.
- 5-day repeat
- Primary uses: land cover, agriculture, vegetation monitoring, water quality, urban mapping
![[Pasted image 20260419210224.png]]

==Sentinel-3==: Ocean and Land Color
- Two satellites: 3A (2016) and 3B (2018)
- Two main instruments:
	- OCLI (Ocean and Land Color Instrument): 21 bands, 300m resolution, designed for ocean color, phytoplankton, water quality
	- SLSTR (Sea and Land Surface Temperature Radiometer): [[Thermal Infrared]], for sea surface temperature, land surface temperature, 500m-1km resolution.

==Sentinel-4== (2025)
- Main mission is to monitor atmospheric chemistry from [[Geostationary Orbit|Geostationary]] orbit; air quality monitoring over Europe.

==Sentinel-5== (2025)
- Main mission is atmospheric chemistry; the most capable atmospheric chemistry sensor ever flown on a free-access satellite. Daily global coverage.

==Sentinel-6== (2020)
- Ocean Altimetry; Radar altimeter measures sea surface height with millimeter precision. Primary use is sea level rise monitoring, ocean circulation.



The missions Sentinel-2A and Sentinel-2B satellites were joined in orbit in 2024 by a third, Sentinel-2C, and in the future by a Sentinel-2D, which will eventually replace the A and B satellites, respectively.

Supports agricultural monitoring, emergencies management, land cover classification, and water quality.

Developed and operated by the [[European Space Agency]].


Mission:
- Multispectral data in 13 bands in visible, near infrared, and short wave infrared.
- Global coverage of land surfaces from 56S to 84N, coastal waters, and all of the Mediterranean sea.
- Revisiting every 10 days under the same viewing angles.
- Spatial resolution of 10m, 20m, and 60m.
- 290km field of view
- Free and open data policy
