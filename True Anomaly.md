An [[Orbital Elements|Orbital Element]], part of a set of parameters describing the orbit of an object around a central body.

The angle that tells where an orbiting object is along its orbit, at a specific moment, as an angular position.
It is measured in the orbital plane, from [[Periapsis]]  to the object's current position, with the central body at the angle's vertex.

> *"Plainly: true anomaly is the satellite’s current angular position on its orbital path."*

For an Earth orbit:
- 0 Degree True Anomaly: The satellite is at [[Periapsis|Perigee]], the closest point to Earth.
- 180 Degree True Anomaly: The satellite is at [[Apigee]], the furthest point from Earth.
- Between 0 and 360, the satellite is somewhere else along the orbit.
