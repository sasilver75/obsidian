The component of a machine or system that is ==responsible for moving and controlling a mechanism.== 
- Essentially acts as the mechanical "muscle=" ==converts an energy input== (e.g. electricity, hydraulic fuel, or compressed air) ==into physical force and motion==.
- They translate control signals (from computer or human) into tangible action. 
	- When a thermostat detects a cold room, it signals an actuator to open a heating valve.
- It is a type of [[Transducer]].

> "An actuator is responsible for converting the digital world of signals into the physical world of position. Made of a motor, gearbox, a motor controller."

Types:
- ==Electric Actuator==: Convert electrical energy into mechanical movement using motors. Widely used in robotics, adjustable furniture, or industrial automation due to high precision and clean operation.
- ==Pneumatic Actuator==: Powered by compressed air or gas; highly highly responsive and used for factory automation and large-scale industrial valves.
- ==Hydraulic Actuator==: Use pressurized, incompressible liquid (like oil) to generate significant force. There are common in heavy machinery like excavators or bulldozers.

Types of Motion:
- ==Linear Actuators==: Create straight-line motion (pushing or pulling), similar to a piston.
- ==Rotary Actuators==: Create circular, turning motion, used for steering mechanisms or opening and closing valves.
