Five major weapon systems developed and fielded in the late 1970s and 1980s that revolutionized US military dominance:
1. M1 Abrams: A heavily armored, fast main battle tank with 120mm canon and advanced turbine engine.
2. M2 Bradley: A tracked infantry fighting vehicle that can transport infantry while providing fires support.
3. AH-64 Apache: A primary attack helicopter heavily utilized for anti-tank, reconnaissance, and CAS.
4. UH-60 Black Hawk: A versatile, multi-mission utility and troop-transport helicopter
5. Patriot: Long-range all-weather air defense missile system

Conceived post-Vietnam to counter Soviet technological advantages in Europe, these systems defined modern ground warfare and the "[[United States Army Training and Doctrine Command|AirLand Battle]]" doctrine from [[United States Army Training and Doctrine Command|TRADOC]].
