Joining metals by melting a filler metal above soldering temperature but below the base metal's melting points.


c.f. [[Bonding]]
- Bonding uses adhesive or chemical interfaces, while Brazing uses a metal interface.

c.f. [[Welding]]
- Brazing is like gluing metals together with a molten metal filler, while Welding is melting the actual parts together into a fused joint. 
- Using Brazing when you need lower heat, less distortion, or dissimilar-metal joining, and use welding when you need high-strength structural joints, and the material can tolerate the heat.
