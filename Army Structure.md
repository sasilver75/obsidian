The official force management and design plan that outlines how the Army will organize, equip, and staff its units over a multi-year period.

Details how the service transitions units to meet modern threats, add new capabilities, and reallocates a fixed number of personnel across the force.

Context:
- In 2005, the Army transitioned from a [[Division]]-centric force to a modular [[Brigade Combat Team]]-centered force to succeed in stability/[[Counter-Insurgency|COIN]] operations.
- In 2018, the Army published the "Multi-Domain Operations" concept. 
- About every 4 years, the army finds itself in a time of major transformation.
- The key takeaway is that we are moving back to Division-centric operations; the key difference between the current army and that of 2030 is that we will no longer be modular [[Brigade Combat Team|BCT]]-centric. We will still have BCTs, but be a division-centric army. There will be multi-domain capability from core/theater-level assets that a division commander will have to conduct combat operations. The division commander will task-organize to achieve objectives...
	- One major change to division headquarters is a reinforced G39 section, which lets us account for Cyber and Space domains. 
- Multidomain Operations is the combined arms employment of capabilities from all domains to create and exploit advantages that lead to mission accomplishment across the competition continuum.
