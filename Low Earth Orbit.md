Altitude: ~160-2,000km
Period: ~90-120 minutes
~88% of satellites

The workhorse of Earth observation. 
Close proximity means:
- ==High spatial resolution imagery==
- ==Low signal latency==
- ==Cheaper launch costs==
The ==tradeoffs==:
- Any single satellite has a ==narrow swath==
- A single satellite ==revisits a given point only once or twice per day==, unless you have a large constellation of satellites.
- Atmospheric drag is significant at the lower end (160-300km), so satellites need occasional boosts, or their orbits decay. [[Planet Labs]]'s Dove satellites operate at ~475km and have a ~3 year lifespan before reentry.

Examples: [[Sentinel]], [[Landsat]], [[Planet Labs]], [[Maxar]], Starlink/Oneweb broadband


Radio signals take much less time to reach LEO than [[Medium Earth Orbit|MEO]]/[[Geostationary Orbit|GEO]], making LEO communications satellites well-suited for delivering higher-quality internet service.

Being closer to Earth also lets satellites communicate with IoT devices despite these devices' low-power radios.
