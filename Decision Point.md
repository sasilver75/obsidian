A pre-identified moment/location/condition where a leader chooses between planned options.

In military planning, usually tied to a trigger like:
- An enemy action
- A friendly unit reaching a location
- Supply status changing
- An objective being seized
- A route becoming unsafe
- A unit needing support earlier than expected

The point is to avoid improvising from scratch under pressure. Before the operation, planners say "If this happens, w will decide whether to do A, B, or C."

In a logistics context, a sustainment leader might decide:
- Keep following the original resupply plan
- Redirect supplies to a different company
- Shift from ground resupply to aerial delivery
- Delay movement
- Execute a branch plan

Simply: A decision point is a ==planned trigger for making a choice, while there is still enough time for that choice to matter.==
