A critical meeting used in the US Army and Marine Corps to align sustainment operations with maneuvering combat forces. Typically happens on a [[Battle Rhythm]] once a day, powered by twice-daily [[Logistics Status|LOGSTAT]]s from sustained units.
- It's a decision-making mechanism that integrates supply, maintenance, transportation, and medical support with the operational timeline.

LOGSYNC
- A good question to ask about the LOGSYNC: Did it happen?
	- "No, life got in the way!" It always will. That isn't an excuse, and it doesn't matter. If you think you're too busy, you're about to be more busy once you figure out that you don't have water.
	- "Were the right people in attendance?"
		- Often times you'll get sustainment, but you won't get maneuver folks; is the sustainment [[Battle Rhythm]] nested inside the maneuver Battle Rhythm appropriately to enable the broadest attendance possible that the Brigade [[Executive Officer|XO]] can support/attend. Every 2-4 iterations, the Brigade commander should attend.
- Make sure it's a synchronization, not just a meeting. Often the log meeting ends because the [[Battle Rhythm]] continues, but nothing was actually synchronized. 8-12 hours out, there's a gap in the swing in the brigade that should have been talked about at the log sync.
- Make sure everyone pays attention, not just waiting for their turn in the briefing order.
	- The whole point of getting things synchronized is to have the cross-talk back and forth; things get missed when you're not paying attention and someone says they have an overage of XYZ commodity and you actually need it.

Typically you'll have both:
- Brigade LOGSYNC 
- Battalion LOGSYNC
There are benefits to covering either the Battalion LOGSYNCs before the Brigade LOGSYNCs or vice versa.

The most valuable part of the LOGSYNC is just the dialogue.
