"Everything is related to everything else, but near things are more related than distant things."
- Waldo Tobler, 1970

Deceptively simple statement with profound implications for spatial analysis. It's the foundational assumption underlying almost all of spatial statistics, that geographic proximity implies some degree of similarity.

"Spatial Autocorrelation" is the formal statistical measurement, quantifying how much a variable's values at one location are correlated with values at nearby locations.
