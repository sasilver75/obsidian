A device that usefully ==converts energy from one form to another==.
- Usually, converts a signal in one form of energy to a signal in another. Often times, electrical signals are converted to and from other physical quantities (energy, force, torque, light, motion, position).
- This conversion of one form of energy to another is known as transduction.

Types of Transducers
- A ==[[Sensor]]== is a transducer that receives and responds to a signal/stimulus from a physical system. It produces a [[Signal]], which represents information about the system. This is often then used by [[Control System]]s.
- An ==[[Actuator]]== is a device that's responsible for moving/controlling a mechanism or system. It is controlled by a signal from a control system or manual control.
- ==Bidirectional Transducers== can convert physical phenomena to electrical signals, and electrical signals into physical phenomena. An example is an [[Antenna]], which can convert radio waves into an electrical signal to be processed by a radio receiver, or translate an electrical signal from a transmitter into radio waves.
- A ==Transceiver== integrates simultaneous bidirectional functionality. The most ubiquitous example are likely radio transceivers (Called transponders in aircraft).


Active vs Passive Transudcers:
- Passive Transducers require an external power to source to operate, which is called an excitation signal. The signal is modulated by the sensor to produce an output signal.
- Active Transducers, in contrast, generate electric current in response to an external stimulus which serves as the output signal without the need of an additional energy source.
