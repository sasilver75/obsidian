A regular wave, usually a sine wave at some chosen frequency, used to carry information.

To send data, you [[Modulation|Modulate]] it, slightly changing some property of the carrier wave according to the message. Common examples:
- [[Amplitude Modulation]] (AM)
- [[Frequency Modulation]] (FM)
- [[Phase-Shift Keying]] (PSK)
- [[Quadrature Amplitude Modulation]] (QAM)

In radio, the carrier wave is what "puts" your signal at a useful frequency so that it can travel through [[Antenna]]s and the air. An FPV Drone's video transmitter might use a carrier somewhere around 5.8GHz, and then modulate that carrer with video information.
