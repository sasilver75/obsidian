In the US Army, a Corps is a large operational formation ([[Army Echelons|Echelon]]) that's *above* a [[Division]] and *below* a [[Theater Army]], typically commanded by a Lieutenant General (O-9), with a CSM (E-9) senior enlisted advisor.
- Typically 20,000 to 45,000 soldiers, depending on assigned divisions and attachments.
- Typically commands multiple [[Division]]s, plus Corps-level fires/aviation/engineers/intelligence/sustainment/signal/air defense, and other enablers.

Its role is to command and synchronize large scale operations across multiple divisions

Designed to fight at the operational level, coordinating deep operations, theater-level enablers, joint [[Fires]], [[Protection]], and [[Movement and Maneuver|Maneuver]] across a large area.
