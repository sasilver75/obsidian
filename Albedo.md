A ==measure of how much sunlight is reflected by a surface==, expressed as a fraction from 0 to 1.
- 0: Total absorption
- 1: Total reflection

Key Values:
- Fresh snow: Up to 0.85
- Earth's Average: 0.3
- Moon: 0.12
- Water: < 0.1

Impacts climate change through a feedback loop; melting ice reduces reflection, causing more absorption and further warming.
- High albedo surfaces (ice, clouds) cool the planet by reflecting sunlight, while low albedo surfaces (oceans, forests) warm it by absorbing light.
- Cities have low albedo due to asphalt and buildings, causing them to absorb more heat (urban heat islands).
