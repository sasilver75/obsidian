A UTM zone is one of 60, 6-degree-wide longitudinal strips dividing the Earth to creaet a standardized plane coordinate grid system for accurate mapping.

Each zone, numbered 1-60, uses meters for northings (from the equator) and easting (from a 500,000m central meridian) to measure locations.

Zones are numbered 1-60, starting at the International Date Line (180 W) and moving east.
