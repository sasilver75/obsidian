The capacity factor is the ==ratio== of a power plant's ==actual energy output over a specific period== to its ==theoretical maximum output if operated at full nameplate capacity continuously==.

It ==measures operational intensity and reliability==, usually expressed as a percentage, with higher values indicating more consistent generation.

[[Tri-Structural Isotropic Fuel|TRISO]]-based reactors have historically faced LOW capacity factors (14-40%) due to significant maintenance challenges, engineering complexities, and technical limitations requiring more frequent shutdowns compared to conventional fuel.
