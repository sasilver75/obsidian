A French commercial high-resolution optical Earth observation satellite system, ran by Spot Image, based in Tolouse, France.
- The SPOT orbit is a [[Polar Orbit]], circular, [[Sun-Synchronous Orbit]], and phased.
- Allows the satellite to fly over any point on Earth within 26 days.
- Altitude: 832km
- Inclination: 98.7 degrees
- Completes 14 + 5/26 revolutions per day


The SPOT system includes a series of satellites (through time; not a constellation) and ground control resources:
- SPOT 1 (1986 - 1990): Decommissioned
- SPOT 2 (1990 - 2009): Decommissioned
- SPOT 3 (1993 - 1997): Stopped functioning, decommissioned
- SPOT 4 (1998 - 2013): Stopped functioning, decommissioned
- SPOT 5 (2002 - 2015): Decommissioned
- SPOT 6 (2012 - ): ==Still active==
	- Spectral bands:
		- Panchromatic (450-745 nm)
		- Blue (450 - 525 nm)
		- Green (530-590 nm)
		- Red (625-695 nm)
		- Near-infrared (760-890 nm)
	- Image product resolutions:
		- Panchromatic: 1.5m
		- Colour merge: 1.5m
		- Multi-spectral: 6m
	- Footprint: 60km x 60km
- SPOT 7 (2014 - 2023): Stopped functioning, decommissioned
