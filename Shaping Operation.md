In a military sense, shaping operations are actions taken to create favorable conditions for a main or [[Decisive Operation]]. See also: [[Sustaining Operation]].

They're used to influence the enemy, terrain, population, information environment, or timing so that the main effort has a better chance of success..

Shaping operations can include:
- Reconnaissance and surveillance
- Deception
- [[Fires]]
- [[Electronic Warfare]]
- Cyber Operations
- Information Operations
- Blocking or Fixing Enemy Forces
- Seizing Key Terrain
- Disrupting enemy [[Command and Control]] (C2)
- Interdicting reinforcement or [[Sustainment|Logistics]]
- Building partner or civilian support
- Positioning friendly forces and supplies


Example:
> Before a [[Brigade]] attacks an objective, the [[Division]] uses long-range [[Fires]], [[Electronic Warfare|EW]], reconnaissance, and deception to disrupt enemy air defenses ([[Suppression of Enemy Air Defenses|SEAD]]), isolate the objective, confuse enemy commanders, and prevent reinforcements from arriving.
