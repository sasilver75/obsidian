The designated, fortified logistical hub established by a [[Brigade Support Battalion]] (BSB) to sustain combat operations.
- Serves as the primary center for a [[Brigade Combat Team]]'s (BCT's):
	- Receiving...
	- Storing...
	- and Distributing supplies, medical care, and maintenance support for a BCT.

> *"The central nervous system for tactical logistics, orchestrating a wide array of vital sustainment operations (supply distribution, maintenance, medical support)."*

Key Principles:
- Anticipation: Forecast consumption rates and positioning supplies before frontline units run out.
- Integration: Aligning the logistical support plan directly with the [[Brigade Combat Team|BCT]]'s commander's tactical maneuvers.
- Survivability: Balance the need to be close enough to support with the need to be back to avoid enemy fires.

Challenges in [[Large Scale Combat Operations|LSCO]]:
- Contested Logistics: Lone-range precision fire and [[Electronic Warfare|EW]] make large, centralized [[Brigade Support Area|BSA]]s highly vulnerable targets.
- Decentralized operations: Modern doctrine often requires the BSA to disperse int smaller, mobile logistical clusters to avoid detection and improve survivability.
- High Consumption Rates: Maneuvering heavy mechanized forces requires staggering amounts of fuel; an [[Brigade Combat Team|Armored BCT]] can consume over 56,255 gallons of fuel every 12-24 hours.
