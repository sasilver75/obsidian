The most precise term for what people loosely call "resolution"; The real-world size represented by one pixel. WorldView-3's 0.31m GSD means each pixel covers a 31cm by 31cm square on the ground.
- GSD varies slightly with off-nadir angles; the further the satellite tilts from straight down, the larger the effective GSD.


Seems to mean the same thing to me as [[Resolution|Spatial Resolution]].
