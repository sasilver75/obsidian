Every network device is born with a unique hardware identifier, which looks like `A3:7F:0C:91:B4:E2`, which is meant to be unique for each device.
When devices are connected, they use MAC devices to recognize eachother.

Only use locally in the [[Local Area Network|LAN]]; you can't use it to reach a device on a different network. For that, you need an [[Internet Protocol|IP Address]].
