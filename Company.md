In the US Army, a company is a tactical unit made up of several [[Platoon]]s, usually commanded by a Captain (O-3), with an first lieutenant (O-2) [[Executive Officer|XO]], and a first Sergeant (E-8) senior enlisted officer.

About 60-200 soldiers, depending on unit type.
Typically includes a company headquarters, several rifle platoons, and sometimes a weapon or mortar element.

The terminology for companies varies by branch:
- Company: Infantry, engineer, signal, support, and many others
- [[Troop]]: Field artillery and air defense artillery
- [[Battery (Echelon)|Battery]]: Cavalry

The higher unit above a Company is a [[Battalion]].
