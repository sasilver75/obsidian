cf. [[Lithium Polymer]] (LiPo)

A rechargeable battery that stores energy by moving lithium ions between two electrodes during charging and discharging.

Have high energy density, are rechargeable many times, relatively lightweight, and low self-discharge compared with older rechargeable batteries.



# Comparison with LiPo:
- [[Lithium Polymer]] are typically more common in drones, RC vehicles, compact electronics, and devices where shape and weight matter. Better when you need a thin, light, high-discharge battery in a custom shape.
	- Flat pouch cells using a polymer-style electrolyte or gel separator, common in drones, etc.
- Li-Ion are usually better for energy storage, durability, and standard battery packs.
	- Cylindrical or prismatic cells wit h a rigid metal case, common in laptops, power tools, flashlights.
