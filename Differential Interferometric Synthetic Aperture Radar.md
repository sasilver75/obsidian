While [[Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar|InSAR]] uses two or more radar images to measure surface topography and deformations, DInSAR is a specialized InSAR technique that removes the topographic phase to isolate and measure precise, minute surface displacements (millimeters) between acquisitions.
- [[Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar|InSAR]] provides elevation, while ==DInSAR is used for monitoring motion==.
