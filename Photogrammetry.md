The science and technology of obtaining reliable information about physical objects and the environment through the process of recording, measuring, and interpreting photographic image and images or patterns of EM radiant imagery and other phenomena.

Commonly: ==The extraction of three-dimensional measurements from two-dimensional data==

==Stereophotogrammetry==: Involves estimating the three-dimensional coordinates of points on an object employing measurements made in two or more photographic images, taken from different positions.

==Photomapping==: The process of making a map with "cartographic enhancements" htat have been drawn from a [[Mosaic]] that is a "composite photographic image of the ground." 
- In other words, the process of creating a map by assembling aerial or ground-level photographs, which are then rectified, mosaicked, and annotated with data like grid lines and place names.
	- Merges photographic detail with traditional map accuracy, often used for visualizing lanscapes.
