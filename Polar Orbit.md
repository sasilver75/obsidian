A polar orbit is one in which a ==satellite passes above or nearly above both poles== of the body being orbited no each revolution.
- It has an [[Inclination]] of about 80-90 degrees to the body's equator.

Used for Earth-mapping, reconnaissance satellites, as well as for some weather satellites.
- Near-polar orbiting satellites commonly choose a [[Sun-Synchronous Orbit]], where each successive orbital pass occurs at the same local time of day.
	- For many applications, it's important that changes over time are not aliased/confounded by changes in local time.

==Fun fact:== Launching satellites into polar orbit requires a larger launch vehicle than for a satellite targeting a near-equatorial orbit at the same altitude, because it can't take advantage of the Earth's rotational velocity.
