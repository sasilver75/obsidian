Organizations that manage and distribute [[Internet Protocol|IP]] address blocks and related Internet number resources *for specific regions of the world.*

There are five main RIRs:
- ARIN: North America
- RIPE NCC: Europe, the Middle East, and parts of Central Asia
- APNIC: Asia-Pacific
- LACNC: Latin America and the Caribbean
- AFRINIC: Africa

The rough flow is:

[[Internet Assigned Numbers Authority|IANA]] → [[Regional Internet Registry|RIR]]s → [[Internet Service Provider|ISP]]s/cloud providers/large networks → end users and organizations

For example, IANA allocates a large block of IP addresses to ARIN, ARIN allocates smaller blocks to Comcast or [[Amazon Web Services|AWS]]
