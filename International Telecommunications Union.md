The UN agency responsible for coordination of global telecommunications (radio frequencies, satellite orbits, and technical standards).
- The ITU ==manages two scarce shared resources==: The ==RF spectrum== (so satellite transmitters get frequency allocations that don't interfere with others) and ==Orbital slots== ([[Geostationary Orbit|GEO]] slots , longitude positions above the equator.)

Countries file with the ITU to register their satellites' frequency/orbital slot combinations, operating on a ==first-come, first-served basis== with a "use it or lose it" rule; you have to actually launch in a certain period or you use the filing.

Slot Scarcity: [[Geostationary Orbit|GEO]] is a single ring 35,786km above the equator. Satellites need to be spaced far enough that their signals don't interfere (typically ~2 degrees of separation for [[Ku-Band]]). This means that only a few hundred usable GEO slots exist, and they're all claimed, mostly by the US, Europe, Russia, and China, which got there first.
