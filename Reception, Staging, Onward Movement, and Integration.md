RSOI is the process that turns arriving forces into usable combat power in a theater of operations.

Components:
- ==Reception==: Receive personnel, equipment, and supplies at ports or airfields.
- ==Staging==: Assemble units, link soldiers with equipment, prepare loads/fuel/ammo/comms/maintenance, and organize for movement.
- ==Onward Movement==: Move the force from the arrival area to its next location, such as a tactical assembly area, base or operational area.
- ==Integration==: Put the unit into the gaining command's plan, command structure, communications network, sustainment system, and mission timeline.

In plain english:
> RSOI is the bridge between "The unit arrived in theater" and "The unit is ready to fight or operate."

Example of a sequence of RSOI events:
> A [[Brigade]] arrives by ship and aircraft. Its vehicles come through a seaport, soldiers through an airport, equipment is matches back to crews, the brigade stages at a temporary area, convoys forward, then plugs into the corps or division plan. 


NOTE:
- In modern near-peer conflict, forces have to arrive ready to fight, as once they enter the theater you have to believe that they'll be under constant observation and vulnerable to long and medium-range enemy effects.
