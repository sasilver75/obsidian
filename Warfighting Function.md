
The US Army defines six warfighting functions, including:
- [[Movement and Maneuver]]: Positions forces to gain advantage over the enemy.
- [[Protection]]: Reduce vulnerability to enemy actions/hazards/other threats.
- [[Intelligence]]: Understand the enemy/terrain/weather/civil considerations/etc. affecting operations.
- [[Fires]]: Using weapons + other capabilities to create effects against enemy forces/systems/positions.
- [[Sustainment]]: Logistics/personnel services/health support to maintain operations.
- [[Command and Control]] (C2): Bundling of the above, letting commanders direct/coordinate/control.
