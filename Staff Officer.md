A commissioned officer assigned to a military headquarters who assists a senior commander by handling planning, logistics, and administration. They manage the complex information/support needed to execute missions.  
- Responsible for transforming a commander's high-level vision into actionable orders, contingency plans, and policies, allowing commanders to focus on decision-making rather than day-to-day coordination.

This differs from [[Line Officer]]s, who lead combat troops.

Staff Officers are usually organized into specialized departments based on their specific functions:
- Administration ([[S1 Officer]]): Manages personnel, human resources, morale
- Intelligence ([[S2 Officer]]): Gathers, analyzes, and disseminates information about the enemy and the environment.
- Operations ([[S3 Officer]]): Coordinates training, planning, and tactical execution.
- Logistics ([[S4 Officer]]): Oversees supply, transport, maintenance, and medical support required to keep operations running.
- Plans ([[S5 Officer]]): Focuses on long-range planning and civil-military operations (often rolled into the S-3 at the [[Battalion]] level)
- Signals/Communications ([[S6 Officer]]): Manages information systems and communications networks to ensure that the unit stays connected.
- Specialized Staff: Includes SMEs like Unit chaplains, medical officers, and legal advisors/judge advocates.


# [[Staff Officer|S-Staff]] vs [[General Staff|G-Staff]]
- Usually used to differentiate these lower-[[Army Echelons|Echelon]] teams from the larger General Staff ([[General Staff|G-Staff]]), which operates at a higher, [[Division]]-level and above headquarters.
	- Again, S-Staff in contrast operates at the [[Brigade]], [[Regiment]], and [[Battalion]] levels.
