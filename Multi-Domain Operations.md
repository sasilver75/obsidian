The Army's current operational concept for how Army forces fight as part of the joint force.

It means coordinating effects across land, air, maritime, space, cyberspace, and the information environment so that the enemy cannot defend against one isolated type of attack.
- It's "combined arms" expanded beyond tanks/infantry/artillery/aviation to include cyber, space-based sensing, [[Electronic Warfare]], long-range precision fires, air/naval support, information effects, and even [[Sustainment|Logistics]].

MDO was adopted in "[[FM 3-0|FM 3-0, Operations]]" as its capstone doctrine in 2022. The focus is especially on [[Large Scale Combat Operations]] against capable adversaries, such as forces that can contest US operations in multiple domains at once.
- The Army describes MDO as using joint and Army capabilities to create and exploit advantages, achieve objectives, defeat enemy forces, and consolidate gains for the joint force commander.

Example:
> Satellites detect an enemy air-defense system; cyber or electronic warfare disrupts its network; long-range Army fires destroy key nodes; Air Force aircraft exploits the gap; ground forces maneuver through the opening and hold terrain.

 This whole coordinated chain is the MDO idea.
