Specific Impulse is a measure of rocket engine efficiency, representing the thrust produced per unit of propellant consumed, typically measured in seconds.
- Indicates how effectively an engine converts fuel into forward momentum, with higher ISP values indicating more efficient engines that require less fuel for a given amount of thrust.


![[Pasted image 20260513175720.png]]
Generally, it's thrust divided by propellant flow rate, so that you know how much thrust (force) you get for every unit of fuel you burn per seconds.

While usually expressed in seconds, it can also be interpreted as the effective exhaust velocity in meters per second or feet per second.
