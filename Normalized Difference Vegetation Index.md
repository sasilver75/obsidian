Remote sensing metric ==used to quantify vegetation health and density== by measuring the difference between near-infrared (reflected by plants and red light (absorbed by plants).

Calculated as ([[Near Infrared|NIR]] - Red) / ([[Near Infrared|NIR]] + Red)
Measures green vegetation density, in a -1 to +1 range.
- Healthy vegetation is in the ~0.6 - 0.9 range.
- Sparse vegetation, shrubs, grassland in 0.2 to 0.5 range.
- Barren rock, sand, snow, or dead/stressed vegetation in the -.1 to .1 range
- Negative values typically indicate water, clouds, or snow.


Used to monitor crop health, detect stresses (pests, water shortage) before it is visible to the human eye, optimizes irrigation, and estimates yield.

Limitation:
- Can saturate in dense canopy conditions, loses accuracy with certain soil types, and is sensitive to certain atmospheric conditions, which can lead to misinterpretation.
