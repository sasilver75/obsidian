A range of the radio frequency spectrum from 12-18 GHz, commonly used for satellite communications.

Why Ku?
- Smaller antennas (higher frequency = shorter wavelength = smaller dish needed.)
- High bandwidth (wide enough spectrum for TV broadcasts and broadband)
- Mature technology
Tradeoff: Rain Fade
- Higher frequencies are more susceptible to ==rain fade==, water absorbs and scatters the signal; a heavy rainstorm can noticeably degrade or drop a Ku-band link; C-band handles rain much better, which is why it persists for critical infrastructure.
