Employs specialized software and a powerful camera system—usually airborne, and for extended periods of time—to track activity over a city-sized area, kilometers in diameter. A WAMI sensor images the entirety of its coverage area in real time. It also records and archives that imagery in a database for real-time and forensic analysis.
- Typically have a 0.5m [[Ground Sample Distance]]
- The typical WAMI sensor produces imagery at an update rate of 1 Hz or faster from one or more multiple megapixel cameras.
- The system then seamlessly stitches together the collected images and applies algorithms to geo-register them, ensuring that the sensor picture represents ground truth.



Started off with a system called Gorgon's Stare mounted on classic MQ-9 Reapers, which used 368 cameras stitched together into a 1.8 GigaPixel composite mage so that you can persistently surveil an entire small city from 25,000 feet; think of it as Google Earth Live.

When something bad happens (e.g. IED), you can then rewind the tape and examine what happened.

A form of [[Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance]] (ISR) sometimes also called Wide Area Persistent Surveillance , characterized by instances like ARGUS-IS.
