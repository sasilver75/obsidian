Wavelength: ~1,000 - 2,500 nm
- Split into [[Short-Wave Infrared|SWIR-1]] and [[Short-Wave Infrared|SWIR-2]]

Longer wavelengths than NIR, requiring dedicated sensosrs (not standard silicon - usually InGaAs or HgCdTe detectors).

Properties
- Penetrates thin haze and smoke better than visible/NIR bands
- Sensitive to liquid water content in soil and vegetation (dry soil looks bright, wet looks dark).
- ==Differentiates minerals and rock types== - different minerals have distinct SWIR absorption features, which is why geologists love it.
- [[Short-Wave Infrared|SWIR-2]] (~2080-2350 nm) is particularly ==good for burn sensitivity mapping== (see [[Normalized Burn Ratio]]); charred material has a very different signature from unburned vegetation.

SWIR is why [[Sentinel|Sentinel-2]] and [[Landsat]] are more analytically powerful than typical RGB optical cameras; these extra bands reveal things invisible to the eye!
