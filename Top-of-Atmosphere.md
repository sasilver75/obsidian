Raw radiance with minimal correction - includes atmospheric scattering and absorption effects.

When a satellite sensor captures light, it's not just seeing the surface, it sees sunlight that's been scattered, absorbed, and distorted by the atmosphere (aerosols, water vapor, ozone, etc.). This is called [[Top-of-Atmosphere]] (TOA) reflectance.
