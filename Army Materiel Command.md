The Army command responsible  for much of the Army's materiel enterprise, including supply, maintenance, depots, arsenals, prepositioned stocks, contracting support, and lifecycle support for equipment.

See also: [[Defense Logistics Agency]] (DLA), US [[US Transportation Command]] (TRANSCOM)


c.f. [[Defense Logistics Agency]]
- Both are logistics/materiel organizations, but at different scopes.
- DLA is a [[United States Department of Defense|DoD]]-level joint agency supporting all services, focusing on common, high-volume supplies (fuel, food, medical, construction supplies).
- AMC is an Army command focused on Army's equipment, supply, maintenance, readiness, depots, arsenals, prepositioned stocks, and lifecycle support.
So if an Army [[Brigade]] needs packaged food, bulk fuel, or uniforms, DLA might be central to the supply chain. But if that brigade needs depot-level maintenance, prepositioned Army equipment, then AMC is central. DLA is joint/common supply, while AMC is Army-specific materiel readiness and sustainment.
