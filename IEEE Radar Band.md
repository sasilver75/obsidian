Letter-designated microwave/radio bands such as (ascending in [[Frequency]]):
- [[P-Band]]: Often explained as "previous," frequencies below L-band
- [[L-Band]]: "Long" wavelength radar
- [[S-Band]]: "Short" wavelength radar
- [[C-Band]]: A compromise between S and X
- [[X-Band]]: Wartime code-name band, widely used for radar
- [[Ku-Band]]: "K-under"
- [[K-Band]]: From German *kurz*, meaning "short"
- [[Ka-Band]]: "K-above"
Note: Not all are strictly [[Microwave]], depending on the exact range used (e.g. P-Band), but in practice they're often discussed together in [[RADAR]], [[Synthetic Aperture Radar|SAR]], and [[Satellite Communications|SATCOM]] contexts.

A totally separate but frequency-overlapping nomenclature from the [[International Telecommunications Union]] (ITU)'s [[ITU Radio Band]]s ([[Extremely Low Frequency]] up through [[Tremendously High Frequency]]).
