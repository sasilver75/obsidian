The "Electro-" prefix just specifies that the detection is done electronically (via a CCD or CMOS sensor converting photos to electrical signals), as opposed to old photographic film. Every modern satellite works this way, so EO and Optical mean the same thing in practice, but EO is the more technically precise term. You'll often see **EO/IR** (electro-optical/infrared) as a paired term, to distinguish this class of sensors from [[Synthetic Aperture Radar|SAR]] or other active sensors.
