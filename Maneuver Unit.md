A foundational military formation (infantry, armor, aviation, special operations, etc.) designed to move across the battlefield, engage the enemy, and seize territory.

They are the primary offensive/defensive force on the battlefield, supported by other assets like artillery, intelligence, and logistics.

Relevant echelons, typically:
- [[Army Formations|Squad]]: 4-12 soliders
- [[Army Formations|Platoon]]: 16-40 soldiers, led by a lieutenant
- [[Army Formations|Company]]/[[Army Formations|Troop]]/[[Army Formations|Battery]]: 60-200 soldiers, led by a captain
- [[Army Formations|Battalion]]/[[Army Formations|Squadron]]: 300 to 1,000 soldiers, led by a lieutenant colonel
- [[Army Formations|Brigade]]/[[Army Formations|Regiment]]: 1,500 to 4,000 soldiers, led by a colonel
