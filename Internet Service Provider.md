A company or organization that gives user access to the internet
- Comcast/Xfinity Verizon/Verizon/AT&T/Spectrum/T-Mobile and local fiber providers

An ISP usually provides:
- A connection to the Internet, such as fiber, cable, DSL, satellite, or cellular
- An IP address for your home, phone, or business network.
- Routing of your traffic to other networks on the Internet.
- Sometimes extras like [[Domain Name Service|DNS]] service, email, routers/modems, or security filtering.


The rough flow is:

[[Internet Assigned Numbers Authority|IANA]] → [[Regional Internet Registry|RIR]]s → [[Internet Service Provider|ISP]]s/cloud providers/large networks → end users and organizations

For example, IANA allocates a large block of IP addresses to ARIN, ARIN allocates smaller blocks to Comcast or [[Amazon Web Services|AWS]]
