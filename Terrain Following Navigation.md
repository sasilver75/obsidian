
A technology that allows aircraft, drones, and or autonomous vehicles to maintain a constant altitude above ground level (AGL), automatically adjusting to the natural contours of the landscape.

Uses a combination of on-board sensors and real-time digital elevation models (DEMs) to dynamically control the vehicle's vertical flight path.
- Sensors: Devices like [[Radar]] altimeters or [[Light Detection and Ranging|LiDAR]] and ultrasonic sensors, constantly measuring the exact distance to the ground or vegetation below.
- Elevation Data: Pre-programmed flight paths use topographical maps to anticipate approaching hills and valley, instructing the vehicles flight controller to ascend or descend.
