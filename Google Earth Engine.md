Compare with:
- [[Earth on AWS]]
- [[Microsoft Planetary Computer]]
(GEE is somewhat different in that it brings compute together with the data, in an optional notebook environment; the others are more just data portals; MPC used to have a "Hub" for this, but no longer)

What it is:
- A massive catalog of satellite imagery and geospatial datasets ([[Landsat]], [[Sentinel]], [[Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer|MODIS]], etc.) going back decades
- A cloud computing environment where you can run analysis directly on that data without downloading it
- An API (JS and Python) for writing geospatial analysis that runs on Google's infrastructure.


![[Pasted image 20260424174418.png]]
- Better for EO experts than for AI experts; you can do anything you want, except Deep Learning. If you want to experiment with fancy methods like deep learning, you can't really do it in GEE.
- Better for educators, researchers who get it for free, than for industry folks that have to pay for it.
- For classical ML and time series, it's great; if you just want to prototype, doing a simple script in GEE is the way to go.
