Altitude: ~400-1000 km (a subset of [[Low Earth Orbit]]
Inclination: ~97-98 degrees (slightly retrograde)

An LEO subtype, but important enough to understand separately. The orbital plane precesses at exactly the same rate that Earth orbits the sun (~1 degree a day), so the satellite always crosses the equator at the same local solar time.
- This means ==consistent lighting conditions across all images==, which is critical for ==change detection==, since you're not confusing shadow changes with land-cover changes.

Almost all *optical* Earth-observation satellites ([[Sentinel|Sentinel-2]], [[Landsat]], [[SPOT]], [[PlanetScope]]). ==It's the default orbit for remote sensing.==
