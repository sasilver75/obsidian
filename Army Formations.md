- Fire team
  - Rough size: 4-5 soldiers
  - Usually led by: sergeant or corporal
  - Plain-English meaning: smallest infantry building block.

- Squad
  - Rough size: 8-16 soldiers
  - Usually led by: staff sergeant
  - Plain-English meaning: a few fire teams; basic small-unit maneuver element.

- Section
  - Rough size: varies
  - Usually led by: noncommissioned officer (NCO)
  - Plain-English meaning: similar scale to a squad, often organized around equipment such as mortars, vehicles, or guns.

- Platoon
  - Rough size: 16-44+ soldiers
  - Usually led by: lieutenant
  - Plain-English meaning: several squads or sections; usually the smallest unit led by a commissioned officer.

- Company
  - Rough size: 60-200 soldiers
  - Usually led by: captain
  - Plain-English meaning: several platoons plus headquarters; a basic tactical unit that can often operate on its own.

- Battery
  - Rough size: company equivalent
  - Usually led by: captain
  - Plain-English meaning: artillery version of a company.

- Troop
  - Rough size: company equivalent
  - Usually led by: captain
  - Plain-English meaning: cavalry version of a company.

- Battalion
  - Rough size: 300-1,000 soldiers
  - Usually led by: lieutenant colonel
  - Plain-English meaning: several companies plus headquarters and support; a major tactical and administrative unit.

- Squadron
  - Rough size: battalion equivalent
  - Usually led by: lieutenant colonel
  - Plain-English meaning: cavalry or reconnaissance version of a battalion; not the Air Force meaning.

- Regiment
  - Rough size: varies
  - Usually led by: colonel
  - Plain-English meaning: historically several battalions; in today's U.S. Army often more about tradition and lineage, with exceptions.

- Brigade
  - Rough size: 1,500-4,000+ soldiers
  - Usually led by: colonel
  - Plain-English meaning: several battalions or squadrons plus support; often the main deployable building block.

- Brigade Combat Team (BCT)
  - Rough size: about 4,000 soldiers
  - Usually led by: colonel
  - Plain-English meaning: a self-contained brigade with infantry or armor, artillery, engineers, reconnaissance, logistics, and headquarters.

- Division
  - Rough size: 10,000-16,000 soldiers
  - Usually led by: major general
  - Plain-English meaning: several brigades plus major support and command functions.

- Corps
  - Rough size: 20,000-45,000+ soldiers
  - Usually led by: lieutenant general
  - Plain-English meaning: several divisions or brigades; manages large campaigns.

- Field army / theater army
  - Rough size: very large
  - Usually led by: general or lieutenant general
  - Plain-English meaning: high-level command for a theater or major regional operation.
