The Army's capstone doctrinal manual for military operations, outlining how the Army forces contribute landpower to the joint force and integrate air/land/sea/space/cyber capabilities to achieve military objectives and win conflicts.
- "Field Manual 3-0: Operations" is a capstone doctrinal publication providing the blueprint for how the Army trains, deters, and fights in [[Large Scale Combat Operations|LSCO]].

FM 3-0:
- Codifies [[Multi-Domain Operations]] (MDO), focusing heavily on [[Large Scale Combat Operations]] (LSCO) against peer adversaries.
- A professional guide for commanders, staffs, and leaders of theater armies, corps, divisions, and brigades.
- Rests on the combined application of Army and joint capabilities, with imperatives including constant observation, convergence of forces, and dominating the [[Electromagnetic Spectrum]].

Terms:
- ==Defeat== is possible without destroying the entirety of the enemy you're facing. You can defeat an enemy just by causing them to rethink what they're going to do.
- ==Defeat in detail==: Similar to metaphor about eating an elephant one bite at a time. You won't defeat China in a single battle or campaign, but you can defeat them over time, accruing an advantage, winning in different locations.
- ==Defeat mechanism==: Method through which friendly forces accomplish their mission against enemy opposition.
	- ==Dislocate==:
	- ==Isolate==:
	- ==Disintegrate==: Disrupt the enemy's [[Command and Control|C2]], degrading the synchronization/cohesion of its operations.
	- ==Destroy==: You're not able to destroy an entire enemy army; it's not possible from a munitions perspective, probably. But destruction is still super important; the lower the echelon, the more important this task is.

Q: Can you clarify the role of the [[Division]]?
A: We say that it's the principal tactical formation in [[Large Scale Combat Operations|LSCO]]. The Division is the first echelon that has access to those multi-domain capabilities, who can then allocate them down to the lower tactical echelons, and deploy them to attain success in the close fight.
This doesn't mean they're the the most important all the time. If [[Corps]] is the headquarters that achieves convergence to enable [[Division]] freedom of action...But the Division is the sweet spot that reconciles close operations with rear/support operations, and brings it up to the echelons that are concerned with campaigning and operations over time on behalf of the joint force commanders.
So Divisions being the principal tactical formation... it means that [[Brigade Combat Team|BCT]]s aren't the only thing that matters anymore, because all the multifunctional brigades and battalions that enable BCTs... that's the level where it comes to fruition. LSCOs are an echelon-above-Brigade fight. You need to be able to coordinate forces in time and space in a coherent and synchronized manner, in a multidomain manner.


Q: What are your thoughts on adding Information as a separate [[Warfighting Function]]?
A: We thought information was too important to be the purview of one community. It's the enabling function for every other Warfighting Function. All Army capabilities have an information component, and need to think about information. We didn't see a need for that (adding Information as a Warfighting Function).
