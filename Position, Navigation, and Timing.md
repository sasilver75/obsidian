Position, Navigation, and Timing: The three core capabilities provided by [[Global Navigation Satellite System|GNSS]] systems like [[Global Positioning System|GPS]]:
- ==Positioning==: Where something is (latitude, longitude, altitude)
- ==Navigation==: Getting from one location to another (routing, guidance)
- ==Timing==: Extremely precise time synchronization (nanosecond-level accuracy)

Why timing matters: Critical for infrastructure for:
- Telecommunications: Cell tower synchronization, 5G handoffs
- Financial systems: Timestamping trades, fraud detection
- Power grids: Synchronizing AC current phase across the grid
- Internet infrastructure: [[Network Time Protocol]] (NTP) servers, distributed systems

Why it's a big deal
- PNT is considered critical national infrastructure, because GPS jamming or spoofing can disrupt finance/power/comms/mil operations simultaneously!
