Primarily a historical and administrative parent organization for lineage and traditions, e.g. the 75th Ranger Regiment.

Regiments still exist, but rarely deploy to the battlefield as a single, unified entity in the modern era.

The most common structural component of a Regiment is a [[Battalion]] (e.g. 1/75, 2/75, 3/75 battalions "batts" in Ranger Regiment), though regiments aren't always composed of Battalions.
- In Cavalry and Armor Regiments (e.g. 3rd Cavalry regiment), the structural equivalent of a Battalion is called a [[Squadron]].
